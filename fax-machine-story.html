She Said Fax Only. He Said Bet.

A Florida man purchases a $15 Panasonic fax machine off Facebook Marketplace after receiving an analog ultimatum. MagicJack has been ordered. Ink is in transit. The situation is developing.

By Staff Reporters • Published April 6, 2026 • Updated continuously

FLORIDA — When Nicole offered to send Eddie a picture of her butt, most men in 2026 would have expected an iMessage, a Snapchat, perhaps even an artful AirDrop. Nicole had other plans.

"Fax only," she told him.

She was not joking. Or maybe she was. It didn't matter. Eddie was already on Facebook Marketplace.

By 10:01 AM that Friday morning, Eddie was already in negotiations on Facebook Marketplace. By 5:35 PM the following day, he had driven to the seller's house — Pat, a nice older lady — and walked away with a Panasonic KX-FP101, a plain paper fax machine and copier, vintage beige, fully functional, for $15. The price sticker, a small yellow circle with "$15" written in marker, is still affixed to the machine. He has no intention of removing it.

"She matched my energy with the offer. I matched hers with the purchase." — Eddie, fax machine owner

The machine, however, presents a modern logistical challenge: it requires a phone line. Eddie does not have a landline. He does not know anyone under 40 who has a landline. So he did what any resourceful man in his position would do — he ordered a MagicJack on April 3rd at 10:23 AM, one minute before ordering replacement ink.

For readers under 30: MagicJack is a device that provides a phone line through your internet connection. For readers under 20: a phone line is how people used to talk to each other before texting. For readers under 15: talking is when you use your mouth to communicate instead of your thumbs.

The MagicJack arrived on April 5th at 1:10 PM. The replacement ink — a Panasonic KX-FA55 Fax Ink Replacement Film Cartridge, 2-roll value pack, genuine — has not. The fax machine sits on Eddie's desk, plugged in but unconnected. Waiting. Like all of us.

Nicole, reached for comment, reportedly responded: "I can't believe he actually bought one."

She has not yet retracted the offer.
