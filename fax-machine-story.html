Fax Me That Thang

A Florida man purchases a $15 Panasonic fax machine off Facebook Marketplace after receiving an analog ultimatum. MagicJack has been ordered. Ink is in transit. The situation is developing.

By Staff Reporters • Published April 6, 2026 • Updated continuously

FLORIDA — When Francesca offered to send Eddie a picture of her butt, most men in 2026 would have expected an iMessage, a Snapchat, perhaps even an artful AirDrop. Francesca had other plans.

"Fax only," she told him.

There was a caveat, though. She didn't want some sleek, modern fax machine — no Brother MFC with WiFi and a touchscreen, no all-in-one that scans to the cloud. That would defeat the entire purpose. A fancy new fax machine would produce something too close to a text message, too clean, too digital. She wanted the real thing. The old experience. The thermal hum. The warped lines. The slightly off-center transmission that says this traveled through a phone line to get to you. For the plot, Eddie assumed.

She was not joking. Or maybe she was. It didn't matter. Eddie was already on Facebook Marketplace, filtering by oldest listings first.

By 10:01 AM that Friday morning, Eddie was already in negotiations on Facebook Marketplace. When he informed Francesca that he had begun talks on a machine and had ordered a MagicJack and toner, she replied with what can only be described as a formal concession:

"Ok I must say... you've earned my ass." — Francesca, upon learning negotiations had commenced

By 5:35 PM the following day, he had driven to the seller's house — Pat, a nice older lady — and walked away with a Panasonic KX-FP101, a plain paper fax machine and copier, vintage beige, fully functional, for $15. The price sticker, a small yellow circle with "$15" written in marker, is still affixed to the machine. He has no intention of removing it.

When Eddie sent Francesca a photo of himself holding the Panasonic, grinning in the sun like a man who had just closed a billion-dollar acquisition, she responded with five consecutive laughing-crying emojis and "you are killing me over here hahahaha." His progress updates, it seems, have been killing her softly.

"She matched my energy with the offer. I matched hers with the purchase." — Eddie, fax machine owner

The machine, however, presents a modern logistical challenge: it requires a phone line. Eddie does not have a landline. He does not know anyone under 40 who has a landline. So he did what any resourceful man in his position would do — he ordered a MagicJack on April 3rd at 10:23 AM, one minute before ordering replacement ink.

For readers under 30: MagicJack is a device that provides a phone line through your internet connection. For readers under 20: a phone line is how people used to talk to each other before texting. For readers under 15: talking is when you use your mouth to communicate instead of your thumbs.

The MagicJack arrived on April 5th at 1:10 PM. The replacement ink — a Panasonic KX-FA55 Fax Ink Replacement Film Cartridge, 2-roll value pack, genuine — has not. The fax machine sits on Eddie's desk, plugged in but unconnected. Waiting. Like all of us.

Francesca, reached for comment, has been providing real-time reactions throughout this entire saga. She has oscillated between disbelief, admiration, and what medical professionals would describe as "dying laughing." She has not yet retracted the offer. If anything, the offer has been formally upgraded.
