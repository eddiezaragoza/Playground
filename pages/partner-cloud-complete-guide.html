1. What is Salesforce Partner Cloud?

Partner Relationship Management (PRM) is Salesforce's solution for managing indirect sales channels — the relationships with resellers, distributors, affiliates, brokers, and agents who sell your products to their own customers. While CRM manages your direct customer relationships, PRM manages the business-to-business relationships that power your channel revenue.

Partner Cloud Rebrand (June 2025) Salesforce consolidated its PRM offerings under the Partner Cloud umbrella with three distinct SKUs: PRM ($25/member/month), Partner Ecosystem Management ($50/member/month), and Channel Revenue Management (add-on for physical goods distribution).

Where Does Partner Cloud Fit?

Partner Cloud sits at the intersection of two major Salesforce products:

  • Sales Cloud provides the CRM foundation — Accounts, Contacts, Leads, Opportunities, pipeline management
  • Experience Cloud provides the portal — the branded website where partners log in, register deals, access training, and collaborate
  • Partner Cloud adds PRM-specific capabilities — deal registration, channel programs, MDF, lead distribution, partner scorecards, and AI-powered channel management

Why PRM Matters

Companies with indirect channel programs often generate 30–70%+ of revenue through partners. Without PRM, channel management relies on email chains, spreadsheets, and manual tracking — leading to channel conflicts, lost deals, and partner disengagement. PRM centralizes everything into one platform that both your internal channel team and your external partners use.

Partner User Setup Model

Partner users in Salesforce follow a specific hierarchy: a company is a Partner Account, people at that company are Contacts on that Account, and those Contacts are enabled as Partner Users to give them portal login access.

Account
(marked as Partner)
→
Contact
(person at partner)
→
Partner User
(portal login enabled)
→
Role Hierarchy
(Exec > Mgr > User)
Key Concept: Role Hierarchy per Partner Each Partner Account gets its own branch in the role hierarchy: Partner Executive > Partner Manager > Partner User. This means partners can only see their own organization's data by default — not data from other partner companies.

Sharing & Visibility Model

  • Partners see only their own Account's data by default
  • Role hierarchy controls visibility within a partner organization
  • Sharing rules can be configured for cross-partner visibility (rare)
  • Vendors control data access through profiles and permission sets
  • Partners can access restricted reports — charts are visible but underlying data is restricted
  • Delegated Administration — partner managers can manage their own users (add/deactivate/reassign) without contacting the vendor

5. Setup & Configuration Guide

Step 1: Enable Digital Experiences

Go to Setup > Digital Experiences > Settings and enable Digital Experiences. This is the foundation for the partner portal. Choose a domain name for your Experience Cloud sites.

Step 2: Enable PRM Features

Go to Setup > Partner Relationship Management and enable the PRM features. This activates PRM-specific objects and components.

Step 3: Create Partner Accounts

  1. Create (or convert existing) Account records for your partner companies
  2. Click "Enable as Partner" button on the Account page
  3. This flags the Account as a partner and creates the partner role hierarchy branch

Step 4: Create Partner Users

  1. Create Contact records under the Partner Account
  2. Click "Enable Partner User" on the Contact
  3. Assign a Partner Community license
  4. The user automatically gets placed in the partner role hierarchy

Step 5: Configure Profiles & Permissions

  • Start with the standard Partner Community User profile
  • Create custom permission sets for deal registration, MDF access, scorecards, etc.
  • Enable "Manage Scorecards" permission if using partner scorecards
  • Configure field-level security on partner-visible objects

Step 6: Build the Partner Portal

  1. Go to Setup > Digital Experiences > All Sites
  2. Click New and select the Partner Central template
  3. Use Experience Builder to customize branding, colors, and logo
  4. Add components: Lead Inbox, Deal Registration, Knowledge, Cases, Training
  5. Configure Audience Targeting for different partner segments
  6. Set your custom URL and activate the site
Pro Tip: Guided Setup Salesforce provides a Guided Setup wizard within the Experience Builder that walks you through configuration step-by-step. This accelerates implementation significantly and ensures you don't miss critical settings.

Member vs. Login-Based Pricing

Member-based means each named partner user has a dedicated license — they can log in anytime. Login-based means you buy a pool of logins and users share them — cheaper if partners don't access the portal daily. Choose login-based when you have many partners who only log in occasionally (e.g., to register a deal once a month).

7. Deal Registration

Deal registration is the core workflow in PRM — it's how partners formally claim an opportunity so the vendor knows about it, protects the partner from channel conflict, and tracks the pipeline.

How It Works

→
Duplicate Check

→
Approval Process
Auto or manual
→
Approved
Protected deal

Configuration Steps

  1. Create a Deal Registration record type on the Opportunity object (or create a custom object for isolation)
  2. Build the page layout with required fields: customer name, estimated value, expected close date, product interest
  3. Configure approval process — auto-approve qualifying deals (e.g., under $50K), route larger deals for review
  4. Set up duplicate detection rules to catch overlapping deals across partners or with your direct sales team
  5. Add the Deal Registration component to the partner portal via Experience Builder
  6. Configure notifications — alert partners on status changes (submitted, approved, rejected, expired)

Best Practices

  • Automate the process — no email-based registration. Everything goes through the portal.
  • Set clear expiration periods — registered deals should expire after 90–180 days if no activity
  • Timestamp everything — priority is established by submission time, not approval time
  • Real-time status visibility — partners should always know where their deal stands
  • Use deal scoring (Enterprise Edition+) to prioritize high-potential registrations
  • Clear rules of engagement — document when direct sales vs. partners get priority
Channel Conflict Prevention The #1 reason partners disengage is channel conflict — when they invest effort in a deal, only to find out the vendor's direct sales team is working the same account. Deal registration with timestamp logging and automated conflict detection prevents this. Make your rules transparent and enforce them consistently.

8. Channel Programs & Tiers

Program Structure

Channel programs organize partners into structured tiers with defined benefits, requirements, and progression criteria. This motivates partner investment and allows you to allocate resources to your highest-performing partners.

BronzeEntry level • Basic access
SilverGrowing partners • Enhanced benefits
GoldTop performers • Premium support
PlatinumStrategic partners • Full access

Configuration

  1. Create Channel Programs — use the ChannelProgram object to define your programs (e.g., "Reseller Program", "Referral Program")
  2. Define Levels/Tiers — use ChannelProgramLevel to create tiers with qualification criteria
  3. Set Criteria — revenue thresholds, certification counts, customer satisfaction scores
  4. Link Benefits — tie each tier to specific benefits: MDF amounts, discount levels, lead priority, training access
  5. Enroll Partners — use ChannelProgramMember to track enrollment and current tier
  6. Automate Progression — flows or automation to promote/demote partners based on criteria

9. Marketing Development Funds (MDF) & Co-Op

MDF vs. Co-Op Funds

Marketing Development Funds (MDF)
  • Provided before sales activity
  • Amounts determined by partner tier/status
  • Used for marketing activities to generate pipeline
  • Partners submit fund requests for approval
  • Requires proof of performance (receipts, event photos, campaign results)
Co-Op Funds
  • Awarded after sales activity
  • Rewards high-volume partners
  • Typically a percentage of revenue generated
  • Accrued over time as the partner sells
  • More predictable and performance-based

11. AI & Agentforce for Partners

Salesforce is bringing AI capabilities to the partner channel through Einstein and Agentforce. These features are part of the 2025–2026 roadmap and represent a significant evolution in how channel managers and partners interact with the platform.

AI for Channel Managers (Internal)

  • Partner Data Summarization — Agentforce agents summarize partner performance, pipeline health, and engagement metrics
  • Recommendation Generation — AI suggests next-best actions for partner engagement (e.g., "Partner X hasn't registered a deal in 45 days — schedule a check-in")
  • Einstein Opportunity Scoring — AI scores partner-managed opportunities for likelihood of close
  • Einstein Lead Scoring — prioritize which leads to distribute to which partners
  • Generated Communications — draft partner emails and outreach using generative AI

AI for Partners (External-Facing)

  • AI Chatbots — 24/7 partner support answering product questions, pricing queries, and process guidance
  • Draft Emails — partners use Agentforce to compose customer-facing emails
  • Lead Qualification — AI helps partners qualify leads they receive
  • Product Promotion — AI recommends which products to promote based on partner's customer base
  • AI Search — powered by Knowledge base, partners find answers faster
  • Generated Sales Emails — requires Agentforce Enterprise Edition
Agentforce Pricing Agentforce for Partners requires Flex Credits, not included in the base PRM or PEM license. Generated Sales Emails specifically require the Agentforce Enterprise Edition. Plan your AI budget separately from your PRM licensing costs.

Roadmap (Announced)

  • Reimagined partner UI experience
  • Advanced partner agents for increased efficiency
  • Manufacturing-focused features: inventory visibility, pricing management, rebate tracking

Don't

  • Don't give all partners the same experience (use tiering)
  • Don't neglect mobile responsiveness
  • Don't let deal registrations sit without action

Channel Conflict Resolution

  • Timestamp-based priority — first to register gets protection
  • Automated conflict identification before it escalates to people
  • Clear rules of engagement documenting when direct sales vs. partners get priority
  • Transparency — partners should see the rules and understand how conflicts are resolved
  • Escalation path — when automated rules can't resolve it, have a clear human escalation path

Technology — Reseller Networks

ISVs distributing through VARs and resellers. Co-selling with implementation partners. Joint deal registration and pipeline management.

  • Deal registration to prevent channel conflict
  • Tiered pricing and discount workflows
  • Partner Connect for cross-CRM co-selling
  • Technical training and certification tracking

Complex distribution networks with multiple tiers. Physical goods inventory and rebate management.

  • Channel Revenue Management add-on for rebates
  • Tier-specific pricing with automated discounts
  • Inventory visibility (roadmap feature)
  • Multi-tier distributor > reseller hierarchies

Financial Services — Broker/Agent Networks

Managing broker and agent relationships with compliance requirements.

  • Compliance-driven partner onboarding
  • Tiered commission structures
  • Performance tracking against regulatory requirements
  • Referral management and attribution

Healthcare — Provider Networks

Managing provider partnerships with training and compliance documentation.

  • Training and certification tracking
  • Compliance documentation management
  • Referral management
  • Provider performance analytics
When to Consider PRM If your company generates 30% or more of revenue through indirect channels, or if you manage more than 50 active partner relationships, PRM delivers significant ROI over manual channel management. The breakeven typically occurs within the first year as deal registration alone reduces channel conflict and improves pipeline visibility.

14. Competitor Comparison

Salesforce PRM vs. Impartner

Impartner is the most frequently cited alternative to Salesforce PRM — a purpose-built platform for partner ecosystem management used by companies like Honeywell, Xerox, T-Mobile, Samsung, Okta, and Palo Alto Networks.

Salesforce PRM Strengths
  • Native CRM integration — no sync/mapping issues
  • Single platform for internal and partner users
  • Leverages existing Salesforce investment
  • Einstein AI and Agentforce capabilities
  • Massive AppExchange ecosystem (5,000+ apps)
  • Partner Connect for cross-CRM co-selling
  • Slack integration for real-time collaboration
  • Faster time-to-value if already on Salesforce
Impartner Strengths
  • Purpose-built exclusively for partner management
  • 296% ROI reported (Forrester)
  • Up to 50% increase in partner-sourced deals
  • SCORM-enabled training built-in natively
  • Orchestration Studio for integrations
  • Aimi Intelligence Layer (AI-powered)
  • HyperscalerGTM for cloud marketplace automation
  • Business planning and goal tracking out of the box
Impartner's Criticism of Salesforce PRM Impartner frequently positions against Salesforce by arguing that PRM/Communities was not originally designed as a dedicated partnership platform, that there are hidden costs beyond licensing (implementation, ongoing ops, per-user charges), and that it lacks built-in capabilities that purpose-built PRM solutions provide out of the box.
