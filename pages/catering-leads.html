Pricing Guide

Tampa Bay area market rates for catering -- where you should start

Drop-Off Basic

$12-15
per person
Delivered in trays, no service staff. Client self-serves. Good for office lunches, casual meetings. Minimum 10 people.

Drop-Off Premium

$18-25
per person
Multiple pasta options, 2 salads, fresh bread, disposable plates/utensils included. Sweet spot for most events. Minimum 15 people.

Full Service

$28-35
per person
You serve. Includes setup, buffet staffing, cleanup. For weddings, corporate galas, larger parties. Minimum 30 people.

Wedding Package

$35-45
per person
Full service + tasting for the couple beforehand, custom menu consultation, real plates/linens (rental). Premium experience.

Yasser's Starter Pricing Recommendation

Start at the $15-20/person drop-off range while you build your reputation and reviews. Set a minimum order of $150 (roughly 10 people) so you're not losing money on small orders. For brewery pop-ups, price individual plates at $10-12 and sell directly to customers. Once you have 10+ reviews and photos, raise prices to market rate. People pay for trust.

Permits & Licenses

What you need to operate legally in Pasco County, Florida

1

Florida DBPR Food Service License

Required for any food business in Florida. Apply through the Department of Business and Professional Regulation. If cooking from a commissary kitchen (not your home), you'll need the kitchen's license info too.

2

Pasco County Business Tax Receipt

Local business license from Pasco County Tax Collector. Apply online or in person. ~$30-50/year depending on category.

3

Commissary Kitchen Agreement

Florida law generally requires commercial food to be prepared in a licensed kitchen, not a home kitchen (unless you qualify for the Cottage Food exemption, which has revenue limits). A commissary is a shared commercial kitchen you rent by the hour. Pro tip: ask local churches if you can use their kitchen -- this also builds relationships with Category 4 leads.

4

Florida Seller's Permit (Sales Tax)

Register with the Florida Department of Revenue. Catering is generally taxable in Florida. You'll collect and remit 7% sales tax (6% state + 1% Pasco County).

5

ServSafe Food Handler Certification

Not always legally required for the owner, but many venues and corporate clients REQUIRE it. Take the online course (~$15, a few hours). It builds credibility and trust.

6

General Liability Insurance

Most venues and corporate clients require $1M-$2M general liability coverage. Costs ~$300-500/year for a small caterer. Get this before your first big event.

7

Vehicle / Food Transport

No special vehicle permit needed, but you'll want insulated food carriers and a reliable vehicle. Health inspectors may check food temps on delivery.

8

Cottage Food Exemption (Optional)

Florida allows home-based food sales under the Cottage Food law, but it's limited to $250K/year and excludes many food types. Pasta may or may not qualify (depends on if it's considered "time/temperature control" food). Research this -- if you qualify, you can skip the commissary at first.

Marketing Tips for Yasser

How to get your first 10 customers in Wesley Chapel

1

Brewery Pop-Ups First

Call Zephyrhills Brewing and Double Branch this week. Offer a free trial Friday night -- you bring the food, they promote it on social media. If it works, set up a standing weekly pop-up. Instant recurring revenue + exposure.

2

Facebook Community Groups

Join "Wesley Chapel Foodies", "Wesley Chapel Moms", "Pasco County Community" groups. Post photos of your food with "Now offering catering in Wesley Chapel!" Don't spam -- post once, let people come to you.

3

HOA Social Committee Tastings

Email Club Connerton's activities director (activities@myconnerton.com). Offer a free tasting for the social committee. If 5 people on the committee love your food, you're their caterer for the year.

4

Teacher Appreciation Week

Teacher Appreciation Week is every May. PTOs plan it months in advance. Reach out in February/March with a special teacher appreciation package. Schools order for 30-100+ teachers.

5

Sample Drop-Offs

Pack a small sample tray and drop it off at the front desk of office buildings, dealerships, and medical offices. Include your card and a one-page menu. The admin/office manager is your decision-maker.

6

Church Kitchen Partnership

Ask a local church if you can rent their commercial kitchen for prep. This solves your commissary requirement AND puts you in front of the church staff who book catering for church events. Two birds.

7

Google Business Profile -- Day 1

Set up your free Google Business Profile immediately. Add 10+ photos of your food, your menu, and your pricing. When someone Googles "catering Wesley Chapel" you need to show up. Ask every happy customer for a Google review.

8

Instagram Reels of Cooking

Film yourself making pasta, tossing salad, slicing bread. 30-second Reels with good lighting get discovered by local foodies. Use location tags and hashtags: #WesleyChapel #TampaBayCatering #FreshPasta

9

The "First 5" Discount

Offer your first 5 customers 20% off in exchange for a Google review + permission to photograph the event. You're buying reviews and portfolio photos -- worth more than the discount.

10

Nextdoor App

Nextdoor is huge in Wesley Chapel's suburban neighborhoods. Create a business profile and post your menu. Neighbors recommend caterers to each other constantly for birthday parties and block parties.

11

Sports Team Pasta Parties

Soccer and football teams do "pasta parties" before big games -- it's literally a tradition. Contact the Wesley Chapel Athletic Association and offer team dinner packages at $8-10/person for kids.

12

Referral Program

After every event, give the host 3 business cards and say "If anyone asks who catered, send them my way -- I'll give you $25 off your next order." Word of mouth is king in the suburbs.
