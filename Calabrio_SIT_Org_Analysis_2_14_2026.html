Executive Summary

Org Overview

Calabrio is a workforce engagement management (WEM) software company selling call center quality management, workforce management, and analytics solutions. Their Salesforce org supports the full lead-to-revenue lifecycle across cloud, on-premises, and government cloud deployment models, serving 510 internal users (Sales, Support, Finance, Technology teams) and 31,239 community portal users (customers and partners accessing self-service support and knowledge).

How the Org Works

The org operates as a heavily integrated quote-to-cash platform built on Salesforce CPQ + Billing. The business process flows through:

  1. Lead Capture & Routing — Leads are scored via 6sense, routed by LeanData, and enriched by D&B Connect. Sales regions/territories are driven by 100+ custom metadata records (SalesRegionSubRegion__mdt).
  2. Opportunity & Quoting — Opportunities progress through a MEDDPICC sales methodology with heavy automation (54 validation rules on Opportunity alone). Salesforce CPQ handles complex product configuration with 1,049 product options, 79 price rules, 7 discount schedules, and a custom Quote Calculator Plugin for consumption-based pricing with ramp schedules and overage multipliers.
  3. Order & Billing — Contracted orders flow to Salesforce Billing for invoicing. A tight integration with NetSuite ERP (28+ flows) syncs accounts, contacts, orders, and invoices bidirectionally. Tax calculation is handled via SOVOS integration at the invoice line level.
  4. Customer Success — Post-sale, Gainsight powers customer health monitoring. A community portal (31K+ users) provides self-service support with knowledge articles, case management, and product-specific support regions. Cases sync bidirectionally with Azure DevOps for engineering escalation.
  5. Renewals — Scheduled flows auto-generate renewal quotes 120 days before contract expiration, with subscription management tracking across account products.

What This Means for Optimization & Migration

Strengths

  • Modern automation: 150 active Flows (only 1 legacy Process Builder remaining)
  • Solid trigger framework: TriggerDispatcher pattern with per-object kill switches
  • Mature CPQ: Comprehensive product/pricing model with 8 approval chains
  • Strong integrations: 21 named credentials, 31 installed packages, well-defined data flows

Risks & Cleanup Opportunities

  • License pressure: Community Plus Login at 99% (29,561/30,000) — needs attention
  • Permission sprawl: 357 permission sets with zero assignments; 26 profiles with zero users
  • Automation density: Several objects have 3–5+ record-triggered flows creating ordering and governor limit risk
  • Validation rule concentration: Opportunity has 54 rules (16 inactive) — review needed
  • Deprecated artifacts: 38 obsolete/draft flows, empty trigger stubs, "Do Not Use" permission sets

Migration Considerations

If merging with another Salesforce org, the primary complexities will be:

  • CPQ Configuration — 1,049 product options, 5,626 block pricing tiers, 79 price rules, and a custom QCP plugin with consumption schedule logic are tightly coupled. CPQ migration requires careful product catalog reconciliation.
  • NetSuite Integration — 28+ flows handle bidirectional sync; any org merge must preserve these integration points or map them to the target org's ERP strategy.
  • Community Portal — 31K+ community users with custom profiles, SSO identity provider mappings, and self-registration logic will need careful user migration planning.
  • Custom Apex — 247 Apex classes and 21 triggers with a custom framework; these need code-level review for namespace conflicts and handler consolidation.
  • Data Volume — CPQ transactional data alone includes 110K+ quote lines, 37K+ subscriptions, and 45K+ record jobs that may need archival vs. migration analysis.

Pricing Model

Hybrid pricing with 79 active price rules, organized by calculation event:

On Initialization (Order 5-20)

  • Block price lookup by distribution channel and currency
  • Quantity set to 0 for source amendment lines
  • Net unit price set to 0 for base implementation SKU

Before Calculate (Order 1-400)

  • Consumption schedule rate setting (ramp deals)
  • Ramp date calculation (yearly/monthly)
  • Distribution channel stamping
  • Discount schedule removal for partner deals
  • Payment terms defaulting

On Calculate (Order 1-9999)

  • Pricing Modifiers: On-Prem, Gov Cloud, Concurrent User multipliers
  • Term Modifiers: Different rates for renewals/upgrades
  • Block Pricing: Quantity-based tiers (0-250, 251-500, 501-1K, 1K-2K, 2K-4K, 4K-6K, 6K-10K, 10K+)
  • Exchange Rate: Currency conversion stamping
  • NetSuite SKU: Product master SKU lookup
  • Tax Code: Jurisdiction-based tax stamping

After Calculate (Order 15-1022)

  • Revenue Forecast: ARR, ACV, TCV, MRR, MCV calculations
  • Quote Deal Type: Unattached / Attached / Managed Services classification
  • Effective Discount: Direct, Distributor, Reseller discount calculations
  • Hold Billing: Evaluation for services, CalOne, account type
  • Milestone Billing: 100% upfront for <$10K PS, 50/50 for >=$10K

Block Pricing (5,626 tiers)

Volume-based pricing tiers for workforce management agents and burst fees:

  • Agent tiers: 0-250, 251-500, 501-1000, etc.
  • Usage tiers: 1-5M conversations, 5-10M, 10M+
  • Per-agent pricing with volume discounts

13. Known Issues & Optimization Opportunities

High Priority

  1. Exception Log Queue Pattern Risk: Many flows use Exception_Log__c creation as a trigger mechanism for integration flows. This creates tight coupling and makes it difficult to trace which flows are actually triggered by which business events. The <object> in the flow XML often shows Exception_Log__c rather than the actual business object.
  2. Multiple Record-Triggered Flows on Same Object: Several objects have 3+ active record-triggered flows (e.g., Account has 5+ after-save flows, Quote has 4+). This creates potential ordering issues and governor limit risks from cumulative DML/SOQL.
  3. Mixed Trigger Framework Usage: Some triggers (CaseTrigger, UpdateOpportunityFamilies) bypass the TriggerDispatcher framework and use inline helper calls. This creates inconsistency and makes bulk testing harder.
  4. Duplicate/Redundant Integration Flows: Multiple flows exist for the same integration path (e.g., Salesforce_Address_to_Netsuite_Address, SF_Address_To_NS_Address, Salesforce_Address_Update_to_Netsuite_Address_Update). These appear to be versioned replacements but older versions remain.

Medium Priority

  1. CPQ Custom Script Complexity: The active QCP plugin (QCP_Calabrio_Official_Version__copy_20_02_2025) contains significant logic. The older script Calabrio_QCP_DEPR (5,891 lines with "DEPR" suffix) appears deprecated but remains in the org. Consider cleaning up deprecated scripts and refactoring the active QCP.
  2. Obsolete Flows Not Cleaned Up: 15 flows are marked Obsolete, 23 are in Draft, and 1 is InvalidDraft. These should be reviewed and removed to reduce confusion.
  3. Empty Trigger Stub: CreditNoteLineTrigger has an empty handler. Either implement logic or remove to reduce trigger overhead.
  4. Naming Inconsistencies: Flow and class naming conventions are inconsistent (e.g., Invoice_Field_Updae has a typo, mixed use of _ vs camelCase, Helper vs Handler vs Service suffixes).
  5. Portal Controller Redundancy: Multiple self-registration controllers exist (Portal_SelfRegisterController, CommunitiesSelfRegController, LightningSelfRegisterController, MicrobatchSelfRegController). Consider consolidating.

Low Priority

  1. Workflow vs Flow Migration: 9 workflow files remain with outbound messages and email alerts. These should be evaluated for migration to Flow to consolidate automation.
  2. Test Class Duplication: TaskTriggerHelper_APIsTest and TaskTriggerHelper_APIs_Test appear to be duplicate test classes.
  3. Kill Switch Object: Kill_Swich__c object name has a typo (should be "Switch").
  4. API Version Spread: Components span API versions 58.0-63.0. Consider standardizing to reduce maintenance burden.

What's Working Well

  • CPQ Configuration: Comprehensive pricing model with 79 price rules covering complex multi-channel, multi-currency, volume-based pricing
  • Trigger Framework: SettingForTriggerFramework__c provides excellent operational control for disabling triggers during data loads
  • Integration Architecture: Named credentials and external credentials provide secure, manageable API authentication
  • Approval Workflow: Sophisticated multi-level approval chains with configurable thresholds per deal type
  • Custom Metadata Usage: 15 types with 716 records providing deployable configuration (territory mapping, case templates, product versions)
